<p><p>PVDF, or polyvinylidene fluoride, is a crucial polymer in lithium-ion batteries, known for its excellent chemical resistance, mechanical strength, and electrochemical stability. In the market, PVDF is produced mainly through two methods: emulsion polymerization and suspension polymerization. Emulsion polymerization involves the polymer's formation in a water-based system, creating finer particles suitable for battery applications, while suspension polymerization results in larger particles that can improve process handling and produce higher solid content, enhancing battery performance.</p></p>
