<p><p>The leather biocides market encompasses various types, each serving specific purposes in leather preservation. Phenolics are effective against bacteria and fungi. BIT (Benzisothiazolinone) offers broad-spectrum antimicrobial activity. Oxazolidines and Morpholines provide protection against mold and mildew. IPBC (Iodopropynyl butylcarbamate) is known for its efficacy against fungi. MIT (Methylisothiazolinone) and OIT (Octylisothiazolinone) protect leather from microbial damage. CIMT/MIT combines the benefits of both, while Glutaraldehyde is a traditional preservative. DBNPA (Dibromo-nitrilopropionamide) is effective against a wide range of microorganisms.</p></p>
