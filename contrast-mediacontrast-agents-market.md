<p><p>Contrast media, essential for enhancing imaging quality in medical diagnostics, include iodinated, gadolinium-based, and barium-based agents. Iodinated agents are primarily used in CT scans and X-rays, improving visualization of blood vessels and organs. Gadolinium-based agents are utilized in MRI scans, providing detailed images at a cellular level. Barium-based contrast materials are primarily used for gastrointestinal imaging, outlining structures in the digestive tract. Each type has specific applications that cater to different imaging modalities.</p></p>
